Lesson 10 Social Engineering

Social Engineering

Exploiting human psychology instead of technical vulnerabilities.

What is Social Engineering?

Social engineering is the act of manipulating people into taking actions that may not be in their best interest. Attackers exploit human psychology rather than technical flaws.

Why Hackers Use Social Engineering

  • Requires the least resistance
  • More effective than technical attacks
  • Common in almost all campaigns
  • Businesses face 700+ attacks yearly

Common Goals

  • Money
  • Ego / Revenge
  • Political or social causes
  • Entertainment
  • Knowledge

Types of Social Engineering

  • Phishing: Fraudulent emails/messages
  • Spear Phishing: Targeted phishing using personal data
  • Baiting: Offering free goods/downloads
  • Quid Pro Quo: Exchange of services for info
  • Tailgating: Following someone into restricted areas
  • Whaling: Targeting executives with sensitive access
