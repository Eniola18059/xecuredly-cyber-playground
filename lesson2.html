Lesson 2 Cybersecurity Threat Landscape

The Cybersecurity Threat Landscape

Understanding the range of threats organizations and individuals face online.

What is a Threat Landscape?

The threat landscape refers to the overall view of cyber risks, attack methods, and malicious actors that are constantly evolving. As technology advances, so do the techniques used by cybercriminals.

Major Categories of Threats

  • Malware: Viruses, worms, ransomware that damage or steal data.
  • Phishing & Social Engineering: Tricking users into giving up sensitive information.
  • Insider Threats: Employees or contractors abusing access.
  • Advanced Persistent Threats (APT): Stealthy, long-term targeted attacks.
  • Supply Chain Attacks: Exploiting weaknesses in third-party vendors.

Motivations Behind Cyber Attacks

  • Financial Gain (ransomware, fraud)
  • Espionage (stealing sensitive data)
  • Hacktivism (political or social causes)
  • Disruption (causing downtime)
