Lesson 1 Introduction to Cybersecurity

Introduction to Cybersecurity

The practice of defending systems, networks, and data from malicious attacks.

Why is Cybersecurity Important?

Every second, massive amounts of data are created, transmitted, and stored across networks globally. Cyberattacks can lead to financial loss, reputational damage, and national security risks.

Types of Cybersecurity

  • Network Security: Protects infrastructure from intrusions.
  • Application Security: Ensures software is free from vulnerabilities.
  • Information Security: Protects data integrity and privacy.
  • Operational Security: Policies & processes to handle data safely.
  • Disaster Recovery: Response plans for data loss or disruption.
  • End-User Education: Training to recognize threats and safe habits.

The CIA Triad

  • Confidentiality: Only authorized access to data.
  • Integrity: Safeguarding data from unauthorized alteration.
  • Availability: Systems accessible when needed.

Common Cyber Threats

  • Malware: Viruses, worms, Trojans, ransomware.
  • Phishing: Tricking users into giving sensitive information.
  • DoS: Overwhelming systems with traffic.
  • Man-in-the-Middle: Intercepting communication.
  • SQL Injection: Exploiting database queries.
  • Zero-Day Exploit: Attacks before fixes are available.
