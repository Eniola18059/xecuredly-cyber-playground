Lesson 4: Computer Networks

A computer network is an interconnection of two or more computers that communicate and share resources. Networks are the backbone of the modern world – powering the internet, emails, online banking, social media, and cloud computing.

Types of Computer Networks

  • LAN (Local Area Network) – Covers a small area like a home or office.
  • WAN (Wide Area Network) – Covers large areas, connecting multiple LANs (e.g., the Internet).
  • MAN (Metropolitan Area Network) – Covers a city or campus.
  • WLAN – Wireless LANs like Wi-Fi.
  • PAN – Personal Area Network, like Bluetooth between your phone and earbuds.

Network Architectures

Peer-to-Peer (P2P) – Devices share resources equally without a central server.

Client-Server – Central server provides resources/services to clients. More secure but expensive.

Layered Architectures

Networking uses layers to ensure orderly communication. The most famous models are:

  • OSI Model (7 layers): Application, Presentation, Session, Transport, Network, Data Link, Physical.
  • TCP/IP Model (4 layers): Application, Transport, Internet, Network Access.

Network Scanning & Enumeration

Attackers and administrators use scanning to discover hosts, open ports, and services. Tools like Nmap can identify live systems, OS versions, and vulnerabilities. Enumeration digs deeper to reveal users, shares, and network services.

Common Threats & Attacks

  • Malware – Viruses, worms, Trojans, ransomware.
  • Phishing – Tricking users into revealing sensitive data.
  • MitM Attacks – Intercepting communications between two parties.
  • DoS/DDoS – Overloading a system to deny services.
  • Social Engineering – Manipulating humans to gain access.
